Variants17 One-Shot Learning — Compact Analysis

Date: 2026-05-25 · Author: Cline

Objective

Train from a strict one-shot 17-template digit set, then evaluate MNIST transfer and controlled transformed robustness.

Setup

Training source: data/processed/17digits_fixed_equal_height_thickness.png split into 17 centered 28×28 templates.
Pipeline: src/variants17/generate_variants17.py, src/variants17/train_variants17_cnn.py, src/variants17/label_schema.py.

Class naming

0_variant_a / 0_variant_b style naming is used where multiple culturally common handwritten forms exist; single-form classes use plain names like 6.

Examples: crossed vs uncrossed 0, alternative 1 forms, and crossed vs uncrossed 7 frequently taught in different regions.

Controlled transformed eval set

  • 20 samples/class, total 340
  • Small rotation (±8°)
  • Small stretch (x/y scale in [0.92, 1.08])
  • Thickness perturbation (none/dilate/erode)
  • Low Gaussian noise (std 0.01–0.03)

Results

  • Best MNIST test accuracy: 9.62%
  • Transformed 17-class eval accuracy: up to 99.12%

One-shot performance summary

  • One-shot variants17 best MNIST: 9.62%
  • Transformed 17-class evaluation: up to 99.12%
  • Interpretation: high in-domain robustness, low cross-domain transfer.

Interpretation

  1. One prototype/class gives limited real handwriting coverage.
  2. 17 style classes collapse into 10 MNIST labels, creating mapping ambiguity.
  3. Custom template domain and MNIST domain differ substantially.
