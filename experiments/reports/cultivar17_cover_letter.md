Dear Editor-in-Chief,

Please find enclosed our manuscript, *"CultiVar-17: Three Data-Centric
Strategies for One-Shot Digit Recognition from a Culturally-Motivated
Template Set,"* which we submit for consideration as a Letter in *Pattern
Recognition Letters*.

The paper studies one-shot handwritten-digit recognition from a single
hand-drawn template per class. Rather than proposing a new model, we isolate
the *augmentation process* as the lever and place three very different
data-centric strategies on a common footing under single-template-per-class
supervision: (i) hand-crafted morphological augmentation with a
prototype-embedding model (77.4% MNIST accuracy), (ii) a class-conditional
denoising-diffusion model used as a *learned augmenter*, which
matches-to-exceeds the hand-crafted pipeline at 79.1%, and (iii) an
interpretable skeleton-graph descriptor with a tree classifier (72.3%, no
deep learning). To our knowledge this is the first head-to-head comparison of
hand-crafted, diffusion-learned, and structural augmentation in this regime,
and it shows that learned augmentation can match expert-crafted augmentation
without domain-specific design.

We believe the work fits the scope of *Pattern Recognition Letters*: it is a
concise, self-contained contribution of broad interest in pattern
recognition, with all code and data released for reproducibility.

The manuscript is original, has not been published elsewhere, and is not
under consideration by any other journal. The author has no competing
interests to declare. Use of generative-AI assistance in preparing the
manuscript is disclosed in the paper per Elsevier policy.
